House Rules - Leveling and Advancement

No Experience Bonus for High Ability Scores

The rules state that characters with high ability scores in the prime requisite ability will earn a 10% bonus to experience awarded. Since this is a rule that exists solely to dick over less lucky players, this will not be allowed in this game. Moreover since everyone should have a high ability score and therefore earn this bonus, since everyone earns it, no one earns it.

Experience Cap

A character can only earn enough experience to gain one level at a time. Therefore, if a character earns enough experience to advance two levels, they will earn only enough to bring them to 1 less than needed to advance to the next level. So, for example, if a 4th level Fighter has 31,500 XP (they need 32,000 for 6th level) and earns another 1,000 XP, they would only earn only 499 XP, since 500 or more would take them to 6th level. If they continued adventuring, they would earn no more XP until they train and advance to 5th level.

Rolling Hit Dice

When a character levels, the player may choose to either take the average or roll two dice of the appropriate type and take the higher value. Once the player rolls the hit dice, however, they cannot then choose to take the average.

New Spells for Mages

When a wizard character levels and can now cast spells of a higher level, they automatically learn a new spell of that new spell level. They do not need to roll to learn the spell, and the player may select the spell subject to the DM’s approval. Specialist Mages will learn two new spells, with at least one being from their chosen school. Neither can be from their opposition schools.

Training

All characters will have to train to advance a level. To train, a character will need to seek out a tutor that is at least one level higher than the character’s current level or, in other words, equal to the level the character is training to achieve. The tutor must be of the same class as the character wishing to train, but otherwise does not need to share anything in common. A Fighter tutor, for example, may specialize in a different weapon than his student. A specialty Wizard PC may receive training from a Mage or Specialist Wizard. A Cleric of one deity may train a Cleric of another, assuming their gods are not opposed.

Training will take 1 week per the character’s current level, and the cost will be 100 gp/week * the current level. For example, a 1st level character training to advance to level 2 would require 1 week of training and would cost 100 gp. A 6th level character training to reach 7th level would require 6 weeks and the cost would be 3,600 gp. This is not an absolute rule, however, and players are always welcome to try to work out whatever deals they can with the tutor.
